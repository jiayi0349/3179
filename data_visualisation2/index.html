Life expectancy & Socio-Economic

Life expectancy is a key indicator for assessing the health status of a population. Further analyses are necessary to determine how the distribution of wealth in a country has an overall impact on life expectancy through certain investments in health care, education, environmental stewardship and some socio-economic factors.

Influence of sanitation on life expectancy

Sanitation refers to the population using safely managed sanitation services (% of the population). Proportion of the population using improved sanitation facilities that are not shared with other households and where excreta are safely disposed of in situ or transported and disposed of off-site.

Health expenditure

Current level of health expenditure expressed as a percentage of GDP. Estimates of current health expenditures include annual consumption of health-care goods and services. This visualization expresses the fact that Thailand has always had a smaller share of health spending and Australia has a larger share, but both countries have had smooth changes in this area.

CO2 as life expectancy factor

Carbon dioxide emissions (thousands of tons) - Carbon dioxide emissions are emissions of carbon dioxide from the combustion of fossil fuels and cement. They include carbon dioxide produced in the following processes Carbon dioxide from the consumption of solid, liquid and gaseous fuels and from the combustion of natural gas.
