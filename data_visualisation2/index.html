Life expectancy & Socio-Economic

Life expectancy is a key indicator for assessing the health status of a population. Further analyses are necessary to determine how the distribution of wealth in a country has an overall impact on life expectancy through certain investments in health care, education, environmental stewardship and socio-economic factors.

The three areas that unfold in the visualization below are Public health, the economic status of the population, and environmental factors.

Global Life Expectancy

The map visualization depicts the distribution of global life expectancy counted during 2019, the color of the markers represents the degree of longevity of the life expectancy, the darker the color, the longer the life expectancy of the country.

Overall, most countries have a life expectancy of around 70 years, which is a relatively normal range, but there are particularly striking examples.

The lightest color on the map represents the country with the lowest life expectancy, which is Chad, with a life expectancy of 54.239.

The darkest color represents the country with the highest life expectancy, which is Japan, with a life expectancy of 84.356. The difference between these two countries is almost twice as great!

Public Health

Sanitation Refers to the population using safely administered sanitation services (percentage of the population). Proportion of population using improved sanitation facilities that are not shared with other households. Excreta is safely disposed of in situ or transported and disposed of off-site.

In the Visualization, five nationally representative countries were selected to explore the relationship between public health and life expectancy.

Among them, it can be seen that the best improvement in public health is in China, which has risen from 13% to 68% over time, and the most obvious change that follows is the concomitant increase in life expectancy.

In other countries, public health has risen less, but it can also be seen that life expectancy has steadily increased as public health has grown. Therefore, it can be seen that the state of public health is one of the factors affecting life expectancy.

Economic status of the population

Higher economic levels usually mean better living conditions, including better diet, housing and health care. Countries with higher levels of economic development usually have longer life expectancy.

Current level of health expenditures expressed as a percentage of GDP. Estimates of current health expenditure include annual consumption of medical goods and services. Five representative countries were selected for the study, and this data suggests that Thailand has consistently had a smaller share of health spending and Australia a larger share, but both have experienced smooth changes in this area.

In the visualization, counts were calculated for all countries in the income bracket over the years 2001 to 2019. From the pie chart, it can be concluded that among the countries of the world, the share of high-income people is the largest, followed by upper-middle income, and the lowest share of counts is for low-income people.

But people living in poverty tend to face more health risks, as they may not have access to basic needs such as quality health care, a healthy diet and housing. This means that people living in poverty tend to have lower life expectancy, which is one of the factors associated with life expectancy.

Environmental factors

Carbon dioxide emissions (thousands of tons) - Carbon dioxide emissions are emissions of carbon dioxide from the combustion of fossil fuels and cement. They include carbon dioxide produced in the following processes Carbon dioxide from the consumption of solid, liquid and gaseous fuels and from the combustion of natural gas.

And CO2 emissions can be equally harmful to our bodies. This stacked bar chart continues with five representative countries, with China emitting the most carbon dioxide due to its population and territory size. When compared to the map, countries that emit less have a relatively longer life expectancy.
